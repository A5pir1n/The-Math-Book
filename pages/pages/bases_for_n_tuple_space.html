Basis for the n-tuple space

Let F be a field and in Fn let S be the subset consisting of the vectors ϵ1, ϵ2, …, ϵn defined by $$\begin{aligned} \epsilon_1 & =(1,0,0, \ldots, 0) \\ \epsilon_2 & =(0,1,0, \ldots, 0) \\ & \hspace{1cm}\vdots \\ \epsilon_n & =(0,0,0, \ldots, 1) . \end{aligned}$$ Let x1, x2, …, xn be scalars in F and put α = x1ϵ1 + x2ϵ2 + ⋯ + xnϵn. Then   α = (x1,x2,…,xn). This shows that ϵ1, …, ϵn span Fn. Since α = 0 if and only if x1= x2 = ⋯ = xn = 0, the vectors ϵ1, …, ϵn are linearly independent. The set S = {ϵ1,…,ϵn} is accordingly a basis for Fn. We shall call this particular basis the standard basis of Fn.
