\begin{equation}
\begin{aligned}
& \iiint_R f(r, \phi, \theta) d V \\
= & \iiint_R f(r, \phi, \theta)(d r)(r d \theta)(r \sin (\theta) d \phi) \\
= & \iiint_R f(r, \phi, \theta) r^2 \sin (\theta) d \theta d \phi d r
\end{aligned}

\end{equation}

If you are integrating over a spherically symmetric volume, the integral becomes
\begin{align}
\int_0^R \int_{0}^{2\pi} \int_{0}^\pi f(r, \phi, \theta) r^2 \sin (\theta) d \theta d \phi d r = \int_0^R f(r, \phi, \theta) 4 \pi r^2 dr
\end{align}
